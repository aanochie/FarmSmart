Rice Blast

A rice crop affected by rice blast

Introduction

Rice Blast is a prevalent and destructive disease affecting rice crops. It can significantly reduce yield and affect the quality of the produce.

Causes

Rice Blast is primarily caused by the fungus Magnaporthe oryzae. The fungus survives in plant debris and soil, and it can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Rice Blast include:

  • Spots or lesions on leaves and stems that are gray to white in color, often with a dark brown border.
  • Infected grains may have gray, velvety growths.
  • Severe infections can lead to blighted panicles and significant yield loss.

Prevention and Management

Rice Blast is most effectively treated using either of the following pesticides:

  • 2.0g/kg Captan
  • 2.0g/kg Carbendazium
  • 2.0g/kg Thiram
  • 2.0g/kg Tricylazole

Systemic fungicides can also be used to control Rice Blast:

  • Pyroquilon
  • Tricyclazole
  • Edifenpos

Tricyclazole can be sprayed at 1g/lit of water

Epifendos can also be used, sprayed at 1ml/lit of water
