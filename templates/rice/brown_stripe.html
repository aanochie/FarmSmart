Brown Stripe

A rice crop affected by brown stripe

Introduction

Brown Stripe is a disease affecting the seedlings of rice. It is distinguished by a brown stripe on the leaf in alongside curving of the leaf. Infected crops exhibit browning and drying of grain weight.

Causes

Brown Stripe is caused by the fungus Drechslera oryzae. The fungus can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Brown Stripe include:

  • Yellow to brown stripes on leaves and sheaths.
  • Infected grains may be discolored or empty.
  • Severe infections can lead to significant yield loss.

Prevention and Management

Brown Stripe can easily be managed with common practices involving:

  • Field hygeine
  • Plowing under rice stubble, straw and seedlings which may be infected
  • Provide a balanced amount of nutrients, especially Nitrogen
