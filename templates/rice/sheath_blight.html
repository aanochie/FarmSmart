Sheath Blight

A rice crop affected by sheath blight

Introduction

Sheath Blight is the most economically impacting rice disease. It results in a serious reduction in grain yield and quality. The soilborne disease can lead up to a 50% loss in yield.

Causes

Sheath Blight is caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia solani. The fungus can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Sheath Blight include:

  • Elliptical or oblong green-gray lesions on the sheath that enlarge and become grayish white with a brown margin.
  • Infected grains may be discolored or empty.
  • Severe infections can lead to significant yield loss.

Prevention and Management

Typical prevention of Sheath Blight involves the use of foliar fungicides:

  • Carbdenzium at 1g/lit
  • Propicanazole at 1ml/lit

Crops infected with Sheath Blight are treated by spraying fungicides:

  • Benomyl
  • Validimycin
  • Polyoxin
