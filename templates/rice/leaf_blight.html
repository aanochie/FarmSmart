Bacterial Leaf Blight

A rice crop affected by bacterial leaf blight

Introduction

Bacterial Leaf Blight, also known as Bacterial Blight, is a serious disease affecting rice crops. It is most present in leaves of middle to upper canopy. A rice crop infected show small water-soaked areas, which gradually become yellow, then brown, killing the plant.

Causes

Bacterial Leaf Blight is caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae. The bacteria can survive in crop residues, seeds, and soil, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Bacterial Leaf Blight include:

  • Yellow to orange leaf lesions that expand and coalesce, leading to blighted leaves.
  • Infected grains may have a milky appearance.
  • Severe infections can lead to significant yield loss.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of Bacterial Leaf Blight involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and bactericide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of bacteria in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of rice.
  • Apply bactericides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
