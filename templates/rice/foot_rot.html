Rice Foot Rot

A rice crop affected by rice foot rot

Introduction

Rice Foot Rot, also known as Bakanae disease, is a fungal disease affecting rice crops. Typically, grains are discoloured. The severity of infection is heavily dependent on season.

Causes

Rice Foot Rot is caused by the fungus Fusarium fujikuroi. The fungus can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Rice Foot Rot include:

  • Abnormally tall plants with thin stems and few tillers.
  • Infected grains may be discolored or empty.
  • Severe infections can lead to significant yield loss.

Prevention and Management

Effective prevention of Rice Foot Rot involves good crop hygiene practice and chemical treatment:

  • Clean seeds should be used to minimise the occurrence of Rice Foot Rot
  • Application of Thiram
  • Use of resistant varieties of rice
  • Proper field drainage

Infected rice crops can be treated using fungicides:

  • Hexaconazole at 2ml/lit of water
  • Thiphanata Methyl 1.0g/lit of water
