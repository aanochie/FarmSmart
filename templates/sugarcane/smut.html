Smut

A sugarcane crop affected by Smut

Introduction

Smut is a widely spread disease affeceting sugarcane, which results in significant reduction of cane quality and quantity.

Causes

Smut is caused by the fungus Sporisorium scitamineum. The fungus can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Smut include:

  • Black, whip-like structures growing from the top of the plant.
  • Severe infections can lead to death of the plant.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of Smut involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and fungicide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of fungal spores in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of sugarcane.
  • Apply fungicides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
