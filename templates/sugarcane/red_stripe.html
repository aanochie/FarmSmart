Red Stripe

A sugarcane crop affected by Red Stripe

Introduction

Red Stripe affecting sugarcane crops appears in the basal region of young leaves. They cause long, narrow streaks, which become redish brown within a few days.

Causes

Red Stripe is caused by the bacterium Pseudomonas rubrilineans. The bacterium can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Red Stripe include:

  • Reddish stripes on the leaves and stalks of the plant.
  • Severe infections can lead to death of the plant.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of Red Stripe involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and bactericide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of bacterial cells in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of sugarcane.
  • Apply bactericides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
