Red Rot

A sugarcane crop affected by Red Rot

Introduction

Red Rot is a serious disease affecting sugarcane crops, commonly in Inodnesia and South Asia. It is signified by red and white patches and exhibits a distinct sour odour.

Causes

Red Rot is caused by the fungus Colletotrichum falcatum. The fungus can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Red Rot include:

  • Reddish discoloration of the internal stalk tissues.
  • White patches with red borders on the stalks.
  • Severe infections can lead to significant yield loss.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of Red Rot involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and fungicide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of fungal spores in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of sugarcane.
  • Apply fungicides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
