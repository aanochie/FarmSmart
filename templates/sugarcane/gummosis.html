Gummosis

A sugarcane crop affected by Gummosis

Introduction

Gummosis is a common disease of sugarcane which can greatly damage the crop. It is signified by longitudinal yellow streaks, which brown as the plant matures.

Causes

Gummosis is caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas axonopodis. The bacterium can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Gummosis include:

  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves.
  • Exudation of a gummy substance from the stalks.
  • Severe infections can lead to death of the plant.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of Gummosis involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and bactericide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of bacterial cells in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of sugarcane.
  • Apply bactericides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
