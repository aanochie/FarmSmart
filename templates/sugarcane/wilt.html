Wilt

A sugarcane crop affected by Wilt

Introduction

Wilt is a disease affecting sugarcane crops which is visible in canes of 4 to 5 months age.

Causes

Wilt is caused by the fungus Fusarium moniliforme. The fungus can survive in soil and on infected plant debris, and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Wilt include:

  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves.
  • Severe infections can lead to death of the plant.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of Wilt involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and fungicide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of fungal spores in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of sugarcane.
  • Apply fungicides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
