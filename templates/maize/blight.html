Introduction

Blight disease is a common and destructive disease affecting maize crops. It can significantly reduce yield and affect the quality of the produce.

Causes

Blight disease in maize is primarily caused by fungal pathogens. The most common pathogens include:

  • Exserohilum turcicum: Causes northern corn leaf blight.
  • Bipolaris maydis: Causes southern corn leaf blight.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of blight disease in maize include:

  • Lesions on leaves that are oval or cigar-shaped.
  • Yellowing and browning of leaves.
  • Premature death of leaves.
  • Reduced kernel size and quality.

Prevention and Management

Effective management of blight disease involves a combination of cultural practices, resistant varieties, and fungicide applications. Here are some strategies:

  • Rotate crops to reduce the buildup of fungal spores in the soil.
  • Plant resistant varieties of maize.
  • Apply fungicides as a preventive measure, especially during favorable conditions for the disease.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to improve air circulation.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest.
