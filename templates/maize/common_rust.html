Common Rust Disease in Maize

Common Rust disease on maize leaf

Introduction

Common Rust is a widespread fungal disease affecting maize (corn). It is caused by the fungus Puccinia sorghi. This disease can cause significant yield losses if not managed properly, particularly in susceptible maize varieties.

Causes

Common Rust is spread by windborne spores. The fungus requires living host tissue to survive and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions of cool temperatures and high humidity.

Symptoms

  • Small, round to oval pustules on both leaf surfaces, initially pale green to yellow and turning reddish-brown.
  • Pustules that release reddish-brown spores, which can rub off on hands and clothes.
  • Severe infections that cause leaves to yellow and die prematurely, reducing photosynthetic area and plant vigor.

Management

  • Resistant Varieties: Planting maize varieties that are resistant to Common Rust.
  • Fungicide Applications: Applying fungicides when the disease is first detected can help control its spread.
  • Cultural Practices: Practices such as crop rotation and maintaining good field hygiene can reduce disease pressure.
  • Monitoring and Forecasting: Regularly monitoring fields for early signs of infection and using disease forecasting models to predict outbreaks.
