Introduction

Gray Leaf Spot is a significant fungal disease affecting maize, caused by the pathogen Cercospora zeae-maydis. The disease can lead to substantial yield losses, particularly in areas with warm, humid conditions.

Causes

The Gray Leaf Spot pathogen spreads via wind and rain-splashed spores. It can survive on crop residues, making it important to manage debris to reduce inoculum levels.

Symptoms

  • Small, rectangular, tan to gray lesions on the leaves, often with a yellow halo.
  • Lesions that expand and merge, covering large areas of leaf tissue.
  • Premature leaf senescence and reduced photosynthetic capacity, leading to stunted growth and lower yields.

Management

  • Resistant Varieties: Planting maize hybrids that are resistant or tolerant to Gray Leaf Spot.
  • Crop Rotation: Rotating with non-host crops to reduce the pathogen load in the field.
  • Field Hygiene: Removing and destroying infected crop residues to minimize sources of the pathogen.
  • Fungicide Applications: Using fungicides as a preventive measure or at the first sign of disease can help control its spread.
  • Optimal Planting Practices: Ensuring good plant spacing and adequate airflow to reduce humidity around plants.
