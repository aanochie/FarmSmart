Ergot Disease in Wheat

Ergot on wheat spike

Wheat spikes infected with ergot

Type

Fungal: Claviceps purpurea

Causes

Ergot disease spreads through spores produced by sclerotia. These spores infect the flowers of host plants. Insects and wind can facilitate the spread of the spores. Once the fungus infects the flowers, it replaces the kernels with sclerotia.

Symptoms

  • Presence of dark purple to black sclerotia (ergot bodies) in place of the grain kernels.
  • Yellowing and withering of affected spikes.
  • Honeydew-like exudate on flowers, which can be sticky and attract insects.

Prevention and Management

  • Crop Rotation: Rotating with non-host crops to break the lifecycle of the fungus.
  • Seed Cleaning: Using clean, certified seeds to prevent the introduction of sclerotia.
  • Resistant Varieties: Growing varieties that are less susceptible to Ergot infection.
  • Fungicide Applications: Applying fungicides during the flowering period can reduce the infection rate.
  • Field Sanitation: Removing and destroying sclerotia from the soil and residues through deep summer ploughing.
