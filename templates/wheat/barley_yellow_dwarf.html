Barley Yellow Dwarf Virus in Wheat

Yellow leaves on wheat infected with Barley Yellow dwarf

Purple leaves on wheat infected with Barley Yellow Dwarf

Type

Viral: Barley Yellow Dwarf Virus

Causes

Barley Yellow Dwarf Virus is primarily transmitted by several species of aphids, including the oat bird-cherry aphid and the English grain aphid. These insects acquire the virus by feeding on infected plants and can transmit it to healthy plants as they move and feed.

Symptoms

  • Yellowing, reddening of the leaf tips and margins, starting from the older leaves and progressing to the younger leaves.
  • Stunted growth and reduced tillering.
  • Delayed heading and maturity.
  • Leaves may be red, purple, orange, green or brown
  • Reduced root growth and poor root system development.

Management

  • Resistant Varieties: Planting wheat varieties that are resistant or tolerant to BYDV.
  • Aphid Control: Monitoring aphid populations and applying insecticides when necessary to control their numbers.
  • Early Planting: Planting wheat early in the season to avoid peak aphid activity.
  • Crop Rotation: Rotating crops to reduce the prevalence of aphid vectors.
