Fusarium Head Blight(Scab) in Wheat

Fusarium head blight symptoms on wheat

Partially bleached heads in field of wheat

Type

Fungal: Fusarium graminearum

Causes

Fusarium Head Blight is primarily spread through spores produced by the fungus, which can be carried by wind or rain splash. The spores infect the flowers and developing kernels, especially under warm and humid conditions.

Symptoms

  • Bleached spikelets or entire heads, particularly in the middle or upper portions of the spike.
  • Pink or salmon-colored fungal growth on infected spikelets under moist conditions.
  • Shriveled and discolored grains, often referred to as "tombstone" kernels.

Prevention and Management

  • Resistant Varieties: Planting wheat varieties that are resistant or tolerant to FHB.
  • Crop Rotation: Rotating with non-host crops to reduce inoculum levels in the field.
  • Tillage Practices: Using tillage to bury crop residues that harbor the fungus.
  • Fungicide Applications: Applying fungicides at the appropriate growth stages can help reduce infection rates.
  • Monitoring Weather Conditions: Keeping an eye on weather forecasts and applying fungicides when conditions favor the development of the disease.
