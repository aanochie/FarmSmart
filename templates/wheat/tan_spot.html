Tan Spot in Wheat

Wheat leaf showing sympotoms of tan spot

Tan spot lesion on wheat leaf

Type

Fungal: Pyrenophora tritici-repentis

Causes

Tan Spot is primarily spread through infected crop residues and spores that can be dispersed by wind and rain. The fungus overwinters in crop residues and can infect new wheat plants in the following growing season.

Symptoms

  • Small, tan-colored spots that enlarge and form oval lesions with yellow halos.
  • Lesions that can coalesce, leading to large, dead areas on the leaves.
  • Premature leaf senescence, reducing photosynthetic capacity and grain fill.

Management

  • Resistant Varieties: Planting wheat varieties that are resistant or tolerant to Tan Spot.
  • Crop Rotation: Rotating with non-host crops to reduce the amount of inoculum in the field.
  • Tillage Practices: Using tillage to bury crop residues can help reduce the inoculum levels.
  • Fungicide Applications: Applying fungicides at the flag leaf stage can help protect against severe infections.
  • Field Sanitation: Removing and destroying infected residues to reduce the source of the fungus.
